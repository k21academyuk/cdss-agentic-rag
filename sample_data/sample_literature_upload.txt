Citation:
Lee T, Navarro P, Iqbal S, et al.
Anticoagulation in Atrial Fibrillation with Chronic Kidney Disease Stage 3: Comparative Effectiveness and Safety.
Journal of Translational Cardio-Renal Medicine. 2026;14(2):101-118.
DOI: 10.5555/jtcrm.2026.142101

Study Type:
Multicenter retrospective cohort with propensity-score matching.

Population:
- Adults with non-valvular atrial fibrillation and CKD stage 3.
- N = 8,420 patients from 12 health systems.
- Mean follow-up: 22 months.

Interventions:
- Group A: warfarin-based anticoagulation.
- Group B: direct oral anticoagulant (DOAC) strategy (apixaban-dominant).

Primary Outcome:
- Composite of ischemic stroke or systemic embolism.

Key Secondary Outcomes:
- Major bleeding.
- Intracranial hemorrhage.
- All-cause hospitalization.
- All-cause mortality.

Structured Abstract:
Background:
Patients with atrial fibrillation and CKD stage 3 have competing risks of thromboembolism and bleeding, making anticoagulant selection clinically complex.

Methods:
Investigators used propensity-score matching on age, sex, CHA2DS2-VASc score, baseline eGFR, prior bleeding history, and antiplatelet co-therapy. Time-to-event analyses were performed with Cox proportional hazards models.

Results:
- Stroke/systemic embolism: hazard ratio 0.92 (95% CI 0.81-1.05), DOAC vs warfarin.
- Major bleeding: hazard ratio 0.74 (95% CI 0.65-0.84), favoring DOAC.
- Intracranial hemorrhage: hazard ratio 0.63 (95% CI 0.47-0.85), favoring DOAC.
- Benefit was most consistent in patients with baseline eGFR 35-55 mL/min/1.73m2.

Conclusions:
In AF with CKD stage 3, DOAC-based therapy showed similar thromboembolic protection and lower major bleeding than warfarin in real-world practice. Findings support individualized anticoagulation with strong attention to renal monitoring and interacting medications.

Clinical Interpretation:
1. DOAC therapy may be preferred in many CKD stage 3 patients with AF when not contraindicated.
2. Warfarin remains appropriate for selected patients requiring tight titration or with cost/access constraints.
3. Concomitant antiplatelet exposure increased bleeding risk in both groups.
4. Renal trajectory and interaction burden should be re-evaluated at each follow-up visit.

Limitations:
- Observational design with residual confounding risk.
- Predominantly urban tertiary-center data.
- Limited subgroup power for eGFR below 35.
