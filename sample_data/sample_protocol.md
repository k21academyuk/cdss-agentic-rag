## 1. Scope and Purpose

This protocol provides evidence-based guidance for the pharmacologic management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) in adult patients with concurrent Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) stages 1-5 (not on dialysis). It is intended for use by primary care physicians, endocrinologists, nephrologists, and clinical pharmacists.

**Applicable ICD-10 Codes:**
- E11.x -- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
- N18.1 through N18.5 -- Chronic Kidney Disease, Stages 1-5
- E11.22 -- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with diabetic chronic kidney disease

---

## 2. Glycemic Targets

| Patient Population | HbA1c Target | Evidence Grade |
|--------------------|-------------|----------------|
| Most adults with T2DM and CKD | < 7.0% | **A** |
| Older adults, limited life expectancy, or high hypoglycemia risk | < 8.0% | **B** |
| CKD Stage 4-5 (eGFR < 30) | Individualized (< 7.0-8.0%) | **C** |
| Patients on dialysis | Individualized; avoid HbA1c < 6.5% | **C** |

> **Note:** HbA1c may be unreliable in advanced CKD due to altered red blood cell turnover, iron deficiency, and erythropoietin therapy. Consider continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) or glycated albumin for monitoring in CKD stages 4-5.

**Evidence Grade Key:**
- **A** -- High-quality evidence from well-designed RCTs or meta-analyses
- **B** -- Moderate-quality evidence from well-designed cohort studies or case-control studies
- **C** -- Low-quality evidence from observational studies, expert consensus, or case series

## 3. First-Line Therapy: Metformin

### 3.1 Dosing by eGFR

| eGFR (mL/min/1.73m2) | Metformin Recommendation | Maximum Daily Dose | Evidence Grade |
|----------------------|-------------------------|--------------------| --------------|
| >= 60 | Initiate or continue without restriction | 2000 mg | **A** |
| 45 - 59 | Continue with caution; do not initiate at max dose | 2000 mg | **B** |
| 30 - 44 | Reduce dose or initiate at reduced dose | 1000 mg | **B** |
| < 30 | Discontinue | N/A -- Contraindicated | **A** |

### 3.2 Monitoring Requirements

- **eGFR >= 60:** Monitor renal function annually
- **eGFR 45-59:** Monitor renal function every 3-6 months
- **eGFR 30-44:** Monitor renal function every 3 months
- Educate patients on symptoms of lactic acidosis (malaise, myalgia, respiratory distress, abdominal pain)
- Hold metformin 48 hours before and after iodinated contrast administration

### 3.3 Contraindications

- eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m2
- Acute or chronic metabolic acidosis
- History of lactic acidosis
- Severe hepatic impairment
- Acute conditions with potential for tissue hypoxia (sepsis, shock, acute MI, acute HF)

---

## 4. Preferred Add-On Therapy: SGLT2 Inhibitors

### 4.1 Rationale

SGLT2 inhibitors are recommended as the **preferred second agent** for patients with T2DM and CKD, independent of glycemic control needs, based on demonstrated cardiorenal protective benefits. **(Evidence Grade: A)**

### 4.2 Recommended Agents and Dosing

| Agent | Initial Dose | Target Dose | Minimum eGFR to Initiate | Continue Until eGFR | Evidence Grade |
|-------|-------------|------------|--------------------------|--------------------| --------------|
| Empagliflozin | 10 mg daily | 10 mg daily | >= 20 | Dialysis or transplant | **A** |
| Dapagliflozin | 10 mg daily | 10 mg daily | >= 20 | Dialysis or transplant | **A** |
| Canagliflozin | 100 mg daily | 100 mg daily | >= 20 | Dialysis or transplant | **A** |

> **Key Update (2025):** Per KDIGO 2024 and ADA 2026, SGLT2 inhibitors may now be initiated at eGFR >= 20 mL/min and continued even as eGFR declines below this threshold, as cardiorenal benefits persist independent of glycemic effect.

### 4.3 Patient Selection Criteria

**Indications (start SGLT2i if ANY of the following):**
- T2DM with eGFR 20-60 mL/min/1.73m2
- T2DM with UACR >= 200 mg/g (regardless of eGFR)
- T2DM with established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD)
- T2DM with heart failure (HFrEF or HFpEF)

**Relative Contraindications:**
- History of diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)
- Type 1 diabetes (off-label; specialist supervision only)
- Recurrent genital mycotic infections unresponsive to treatment
- Active lower extremity ulcers or gangrene (canagliflozin -- consider alternatives)

### 4.4 Monitoring and Counseling

- Monitor renal function and potassium at 1-2 weeks, then every 3 months
- Expected initial eGFR dip of 3-5 mL/min is hemodynamic and not a reason to discontinue
- Counsel patients on genital hygiene to prevent mycotic infections
- Advise adequate hydration; monitor for volume depletion especially in elderly
- Hold SGLT2 inhibitor during acute illness, surgery, or prolonged fasting ("sick day rules")
- Monitor for signs of euglycemic DKA (nausea, vomiting, fatigue, abdominal pain) even if glucose is normal

### 4.5 Key Clinical Trial Evidence

| Trial | Agent | Key Finding | Publication |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| EMPA-REG OUTCOME | Empagliflozin | 14% reduction in MACE; 38% reduction in CV death | Zinman et al., NEJM 2015 |
| CREDENCE | Canagliflozin | 30% reduction in primary renal composite endpoint | Perkovic et al., NEJM 2019 |
| DAPA-CKD | Dapagliflozin | 39% reduction in renal composite; benefit in non-diabetic CKD | Heerspink et al., NEJM 2020 |
| EMPA-KIDNEY | Empagliflozin | 28% reduction in CKD progression across broad eGFR range | EMPA-KIDNEY Collaborative, NEJM 2023 |

## 5. Second-Line Add-On Therapy: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists

### 5.1 Rationale

GLP-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) are recommended as second-line add-on therapy when additional glycemic control is needed beyond metformin + SGLT2 inhibitor, or as an alternative when SGLT2 inhibitors are not tolerated. **(Evidence Grade: A for cardiovascular benefit; B for renal benefit)**

### 5.2 Recommended Agents and Dosing

| Agent | Starting Dose | Target Dose | Renal Adjustment | Evidence Grade |
|-------|-------------|------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Semaglutide (SC) | 0.25 mg weekly x 4 weeks | 0.5-1.0 mg weekly | No adjustment needed | **A** |
| Dulaglutide | 0.75 mg weekly | 1.5-4.5 mg weekly | No adjustment needed | **A** |
| Liraglutide | 0.6 mg daily x 1 week | 1.2-1.8 mg daily | No adjustment needed; caution eGFR < 15 | **A** |

### 5.3 Advantages in CKD

- No dose adjustment required for renal impairment (all agents)
- Promote weight loss (3-7 kg average), beneficial for obese T2DM patients
- Demonstrated cardiovascular risk reduction (SUSTAIN-6, LEADER, REWIND trials)
- Potential renal benefits (FLOW trial -- semaglutide in CKD)
- Low hypoglycemia risk as monotherapy or with metformin/SGLT2i

### 5.4 Common Adverse Effects

- Nausea (20-30%), vomiting (5-10%), diarrhea (10-15%) -- typically transient
- Slow dose titration minimizes GI side effects
- Injection site reactions (SC formulations)
- Contraindicated in personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2

## 6. Agents to Avoid or Use with Caution in CKD

| Agent Class | Concern in CKD | Recommendation | Evidence Grade |
|-------------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Sulfonylureas** (glipizide, glimepiride) | Increased hypoglycemia risk due to reduced renal clearance of active metabolites | Avoid if possible. If used, prefer glipizide (hepatic metabolism). Start at lowest dose. | **B** |
| **Thiazolidinediones** (pioglitazone) | Fluid retention, edema, heart failure risk | Avoid in HF. Use with caution in CKD with edema. | **B** |
| **DPP-4 Inhibitors** (sitagliptin, saxagliptin, linagliptin) | Generally safe; dose adjustment required for most agents except linagliptin | Acceptable if GLP-1 RA not feasible. Linagliptin preferred (no renal adjustment). | **B** |
| **Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors** (acarbose) | Reduced efficacy in CKD; accumulation risk | Avoid if eGFR < 25 | **C** |
| **Insulin** | Prolonged half-life in CKD; increased hypoglycemia risk | Use when needed; reduce doses by 25% when eGFR < 30. Close monitoring required. | **B** |

## 9. Referral Criteria

Refer to **Nephrology** if:
- eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m2
- eGFR decline > 5 mL/min/year
- UACR > 300 mg/g (severely increased albuminuria)
- Difficult-to-manage hyperkalemia
- Suspected non-diabetic kidney disease

Refer to **Endocrinology** if:
- HbA1c > 9.0% despite dual therapy
- Recurrent severe hypoglycemia
- Need for complex insulin regimens
- Consideration of advanced therapies (e.g., closed-loop insulin systems)
